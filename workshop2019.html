Detection In the Wild Challenge Workshop 2019

16th - 17th June, 2019

Long Beach, CA

in conjunction with CVPR 2019

Object detection in the wild is of significant value to the Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition community, accounting for a large portion of both submitted and accepted papers. Research groups who are at the forefront of object detection, object classification, scene understanding and segmentation, will want to participate in our challenges and will present their results at the workshop. This will include research groups who have had their paper accepted by the main CVPR2019 track, as well as research groups with new results from work in progress.

Workshop Overview

Objects365 Challenge Track

More specifically, Objects365 (http://www.objects365.org/) is a brand new dataset, designed to spur object detection research with a focus on diverse objects in the Wild. Objects365 has 365 object classes annotated on 638K images, totally with more than 10 million bounding boxes in the training set. Thus the annotations cover common objects occurring in all kinds of scene categories. Challenge for Objects365 is proposed to have two tracks:

  • Full Track. The goal of Full Track is to explore the upper-bound performance of object detection systems, given all the 365 classes and 600K+ training images. 30K images are used for validation and another 100K images are used for testing. To evaluate the performance of object detection, the evaluation criteria for COCO (IOU from 0.5 to 0.95) benchmark will be adopted.
  • Tiny Track. For the Tiny Track, 100K images from the complete set of database is available to train the algorithms for the task of object detection with COCO 80 categories. The tiny track is designed to encourage the novel methods whose performances are comparable to the existing COCO benchmark. Also, it is friendly to researchers in the academic without large computational infrastructure. The same 10K images are used for validation and another 50K images are used for testing but only on the COCO 80 categories.

CrowdHuman Challenge Track

The CrowdHuman (https://arxiv.org/abs/1805.00123, http://www.crowdhuman.org/ï¼‰ dataset is large, rich-annotated and contains high diversity. It contains 15000, 4370 and 5000 images for training, validation, and testing, respectively. There are a total of 340K human instances and 22.6 persons per image from the train set, with various kinds of occlusions in the dataset. Each human instance is annotated with a head bounding-box, human visible-region bounding-box and human full-body bounding-box. We believe this dataset will serve as a solid baseline and help promote future research in human detection tasks, especially in the crowded environment.
